Experience with Problem Solving

Back when I was in college,  I had to make a quiz game using python for an assessment.  I couldnt figure out for the life of me how to get different function to link to seperate functions so after an hour of trying many things I thought of taking this to the internet.  But first taking a littke break to clear my head,  then when I got back I spent the next 10 minutes looking for a way to do this and after finding something that might help I tried it with my situation and I almost instantly thought of an idea to fix my problem,  but I also found a way to improve my code.

What was the problem?
The problem ending up being that I hadnt actually group most things together in class,
so once I did that and added a class I was able to link to other functions
and connect things together a whole lot easier.

What problem soving techniques did you use?
I first used just trying differnt things in the code and seeing what I could think of,
but after that didnt work I took a break to clear my head and tried just googling it.
I also ued thee rubber ducky method talking to a friend to think aloud and it just came to me.

How did you feel thoughout the process?
I knew that I would be able to figure it so once I got in the zone and really wanted to
figure it out myself using my own techniques, I was feeling fine like I knew
I had to figure it out and that helped me figure it out.

What did you learn?
I learnt that its good to take a break once and a while to clear your head and that
speaking the problem aloud really helps,  along with finding out about classes and how use them.
